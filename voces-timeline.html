Voces @ Veinte

Where we've been. Where we are. Where we're going.

  • Dr. Maggie Rivas-Rodriguez wrote an article for Dallas Life magazine in 1992 entitled "Brothers in Arms" about Mexican American WWII veterans. This inspired her to start the U.S. Latino & Latina WWII Oral History Project, now known as Voces.



    Click here to read the article.

    1992
  • The project began in the year 1999 with a goal of creating an archive of 200 interviews of Latino men and women that served during WWII.
    The U.S. Latino & Latina WWII Oral History Project teamed up with the Dallas Morning News and the San Antonio Express News to create the Narratives Newspaper, a special edition newspaper containing articles based on the interviews conducted by Rivas-Rodriguez and the project staff.

    1999
  • After the first Narratives Newspaper came out, people took notice of the project. Volunteers from across the U.S. offered to conduct interviews as well as submit their stories to the collection. Interview guidelines were created, training sessions began, and training materials were produced.
    Rivas-Rodriguez and the project staff organized a conference on the UT campus called, “U.S. Latinos and Latinas & World War II: Changes Seen, Changes Wrought." The project was funded by a $36,500 grant from the Freedom Forum.
    2000
  • By fall of 2001, five Narratives had been produced, with in-kind publishing donations from the Austin American-Statesman and the San Antonio ExpressNews. The newspapers traded off publishing eight Narrative newspapers. The project also accrued partners, donations, and an appropriation from the Texas State Legislature in the amount of $100,000 over two years.
    2001
  • By 2002 the project had support from five national and 12 regional partners. Also, Multiple Individual Interview Sessions (MIISes) were created to collect up to 13 interviews at one location. MIISes took place in FL, TX, MI, AZ, and NM in spring of 2002, yielding 100+ interviews. Most MIISs took place at Vet Centers.
    The project received $35,000 in separate grants from the Cain Foundation, Goya Foods, and Southwestern Bell.
    2002
  • Six more MIISes took place across the US. Talk of holding a forum began. The Texas State Legislature allotted another two year appropriation of $94,600. The project was represented at nationwide panels and lectures. A full-time Assistant Director was hired for the project.
    2003
  • The Narratives newspaper had grown in size from 20 pages in the first issue to 112 pages in the final issue. Professors and graduate students researched themes that emerged in interviews. They made short presentations, with video clips from interviews, at an all-star forum held in Washington D.C. on September 12, 2004.
    2004
  • $8,600 grant from Humanities Texas to develop a video, “Oral History Step by Step,” for the WWII Project website to provide instructions on conducting interviews and submitting them to oral history archives.
    Award-winning playwright/journalist James E. Garcia wrote a play based on the interviews in the archive called "Voices of Valor".
    2006
  • Rivas-Rodriguez and Gus Chavez launched a campaign called Defend the Honor protesting the whitewashing of history in Ken Burns' WWII film.
    2007
  • The project received a $450,000 grant from the Institute for Museum and Library Services allowing the project to expand to include the Korean and Vietnam Wars.
    The project celebrated its 10 year anniversary.
    2009
  • Thanks to the IMLS grant, the project was able to travel to Los Angeles, Arizona, and Kansas to conduct interviews.
    2010
  • Voces launched Share Their Voices, a video-editing contest.

    2011
  • Voces celebrated 15 years with a Quinceñera party.
    Voces collaborated with Austin's public television station, KLRU, to broadcast audio slideshows based on material from the archive.
    2014
  • Voces held "Latinos, the Voting Rights Act and Political Engagement conference" at the University of Texas.
    Voces received $100,000 from the Moody College of Communication at UT.
    2015
  • Voces held Short Courses at Thompson Center, as well as the first Voces Summer Institute. The institute is funded by a 25k grant from the Muriel Pollia foundation.
    Rivas-Rodriguez launched an annual academic journal called the US Latina&Latino Oral History Journal in order to showcase scholarly work based on the archive.
    2017
  • Voces celebrated its 1000th interview with Mil Voces, a celebration at UT held on June 7, 2018.
    Rivas-Rodriguez's oral history class traveled to Hondo, Texas to conduct interviews about civil rights for Mexican-Americans.
